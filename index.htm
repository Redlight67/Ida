Orlando

Mama, me, Turia and Ronnie came to Orlando

XMas Eve or New Years Eve night of 69. Just

like that we were ripped from everything we

knew, brought to Orlando. Four to fifty

descendants from Ida since 1969.

Grownup Ida

Fort Pierce The scenery in this photo seems familiar, 1510 Ave J Ft. Pierce Fl. Genie and Rev. R.H. Pittman home. Florida’s 1945 census places Ida at that residence, identifying her as Ida Pittman age 12. The home was central to us children rearing, my first memories of living took place there. It is good to see mama youthful smile, she looks happy in both photos.

Young Ida

WELCOME

OUR ORIGINS For a significant part of my life my Mother origins was unknown to me. During my childhood I heard this narrative that my mother was of Native American descent, but that never resonated with me even at 5yrs. old. To set the record straight, I took a DNA test and delved into Ancestry records, the U.S. and 1945 Florida Censuses in my quest for the truth. Unveiling the reality became an exploration across the expanse of the World Wide Web, devoid of familial narratives. Establishing we Glenns are of African and European descent, Ida was biracial.
A UNFINISHED STORY This webpage is dedicated to honoring my mother, who faced numerous challenges throughout her life due to her fragile health. Rev. Pittman served as a crucial ally and support for Ida and us children but he move on in August of 1966. It became apparent his absence left a profound void in our lives , a protector. However, I wish mama was here to see the numerous granddaughters and grandsons who will likely carry her DNA into the next century. This platform is dedicated to providing a glimpse into Ida’s origin. Ida was adopted by Rev. R.H. and Genie Pittman after the 1940 Census. The Pittmans played a pivotal role in nurturing her four children, alongside Rachel Bell and Miss Lessie Lake. The mystery shrouding her biological family, stemming from her adoption following the 1940 Census and their absence in the documented records of the 1950 Census, leaves a poignant void and prompts resounding question.
